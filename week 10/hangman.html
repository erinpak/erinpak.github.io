    how does this relate to the reading? (click)

    In the reading, McLuhan argues that technology IS the message, rather than just a medium of communicating messages, and that technology helps us to percieve the world in its own unique way.

    The reading was a bit hard for me to understand at first, but I understood it better with the hangman game example. In this example, the content of the message - the word you are trying to guess - is not really that relevant or meaningful. However, the context and functionality for the game, such as having a set number of tries before something terrible happens -the man getting hanged- says a lot more about the message of the game than its content does. The technology of the hangman game, rather than the content you must guess itself, communicates a clearer message about the dark and hostile nature of the game that uses punishment as a motivator for the user to win.
